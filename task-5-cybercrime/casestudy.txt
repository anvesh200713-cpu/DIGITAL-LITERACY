# Case Study: UPI Fraud via Fake Gaming Currency Portals
**What the crime is:**
UPI fraud combined with phishing is a growing threat where scammers deceive victims into authorizing peer-to-peer payments on fraudulent platforms. 
**How it typically happens (Step-by-Step):**
1. A college student sees a targeted advertisement on Instagram or a community Discord server promising heavily discounted in-game currency for popular titles like Valorant or Wuthering Waves.
2. Intrigued by the deal, the student clicks the link, which redirects them to a sophisticated, pixel-perfect clone of an official gaming top-up website.
3. The student enters their player ID and proceeds to the checkout page, which generates a legitimate-looking UPI QR code or sends a payment request to their phone.
4. Trusting the familiar UPI gateway interface, the student authorizes the transaction using their UPI PIN.
5. The money is instantly transferred to the scammer's mule account, the website disconnects, and the currency is never delivered.

**Who is usually targeted:**
The primary targets are young adults, particularly college students, who are digitally active, frequently use fast mobile payments, and are looking to save money on digital entertainment.

**The consequences:**
The immediate consequence is the unrecoverable financial loss of the transferred funds. Furthermore, by interacting with the fake portal, the victim may have exposed their phone number, email, and player ID to future targeted social engineering attacks or account takeover attempts.



# Stay Safe Online: Cybersecurity Checklist for College Students
1. **The Golden UPI Rule:** Remember that entering your UPI PIN is ONLY required to *send* money. You never need to enter a PIN to receive a refund, scholarship, or payment.
2. **Verify Payment Gateways:** Before scanning a QR code or accepting a UPI mandate for an online purchase, verify the exact merchant name that appears on your GPay/PhonePe screen before hitting approve.
3. **Avoid Third-Party Top-ups:** Only purchase software, game currency, or subscriptions through the official client or verified app stores, never through random Discord links or Instagram ads.
4. **Beware of the "Urgency" Trap:** Phishing relies on panic (e.g., "Your college email will be deleted in 2 hours!"). Always pause, breathe, and verify the claim directly with the university IT desk.
5. **Use a Password Manager:** Stop reusing the same password for your student portal, GitHub, and social media. Use a password manager to generate unique credentials.
6. **Enable 2FA Everywhere:** Turn on Two-Factor Authentication using an authenticator app (like Google Authenticator) for your primary email and financial apps.
7. **Be Cautious on Campus Wi-Fi:** Avoid making sensitive financial transactions or accessing unencrypted personal data while connected to open, public campus Wi-Fi networks.
8. **Know How to Report:** If you fall victim to an online financial fraud, time is critical. Immediately report the incident to the **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal (cybercrime.gov.in)** and call the dedicated **Helpline 1930** to freeze the fraudulent transaction.
