# Social Media Do's and Don'ts Checklist for College Students
### The 5 Do's ✅
1. **Do audit your privacy settings:** Regularly check who can view your posts and limit personal data visibility to friends and family.
2. **Do showcase your projects:** Use platforms like LinkedIn and Twitter to share your coding achievements, GitHub repositories, and academic milestones.
3. **Do assume everything is permanent:** Think critically before posting; remember that screenshots exist even if you delete a post later.
4. **Do network professionally:** Connect with seniors, alumni, and professors on LinkedIn using personalized connection requests.
5. **Do maintain a respectful tone:** Engage politely in online discussions and technical forums; your digital footprint represents your real-world reputation.

### The 5 Don'ts ❌
1. **Don't share unverified information:** Avoid reposting sensational news or alerts without checking official sources first.
2. **Don't publicly complain about your university:** Avoid venting about professors, campus administration, or peers on public timelines.
3. **Don't overshare sensitive data:** Never post pictures containing your student ID, exact location details, or travel tickets with visible barcodes.
4. **Don't use unprofessional profile pictures:** Ensure your display pictures on academic or career-focused platforms (like LinkedIn or GitHub) are clean and appropriate.
5. **Don't engage with trolls:** Avoid getting into heated, public arguments with cyberbullies or internet trolls; block and report instead.
